Introduction

It all started with a simple question: Will we ever learn to think of computers, which will have their own idea of the world?
It will probably be quite different from ours. But, will their vision of the world be worse than ours?
However, what we know about the world is connected with the reality that surrounds us.
On one side we are limited by our senses. We see only part of the electromagnetic waves like visible light, as part of the so-called thermal radiation, from which we get only a minimal amount of information. The sounds we can hear, are only at a certain frequency. We have a lousy sense of smell and our sense of touch is very imperfect.
We have no senses in the perception of magnetism, radiation and many others we could theoretically have. We would totality perceive the world differently if we were blind and had a perfect sense of smell. We could follow the path of motion into the past and we should have a much better understanding of space and time.
We would have a completely different idea about the world, if we were a million times bigger or smaller, faster or slower. If we lived under the sea or on a comet or somehere else entirely.
From the other side of our vision of the world is given a model of the world that we have received from the society in which we grew up.
It is practical to have a stable model of the world and the same with other people with whom we live. But it is good to realize that the company created it based on knowledge that was centuries ago, and it changes very slowly.
The new findings, which do not comply with our world view, either ignore or ridicule, declare a falsehood, fraud or otherwise suppress. We believe that we are right, but if we were born on the other side of the globe, a few centuries earlier or later, our world view would be completely different. And also we thought that we are right.

Maybe everything is completely different than we think. This book does not attempt to solve some specific problems. They try to only give impetus to the development of their own thinking in trying to understand how a computer could think.
I do not know whether we will ever teach computers to think, but if you are going to try, we can not understand many of the new world, but also themselves.
